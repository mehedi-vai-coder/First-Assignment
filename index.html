Dr. Muhammad Yunus, born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, is a globally renowned economist, social entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is best known as the founder of the Grameen Bank, an innovative microfinance organization that provides small loans to impoverished people, particularly women, without requiring collateral. This groundbreaking approach has empowered millions to escape poverty by fostering entrepreneurship and self-reliance. Dr. Yunus's work has earned him numerous accolades, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006, shared with the Grameen Bank, for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. Often called the "Banker to the Poor," Dr. Yunus has also authored influential books like "Banker to the Poor" and "A World of Three Zeros," advocating for a world free of poverty, unemployment, and net carbon emissions.
